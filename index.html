About

Growing up in Guatemala during and after the peace process, I saw the long and often invisible work that follows conflict. It shaped how I understand peace: not as a single outcome, but as something sustained through relationships, systems, and everyday decisions.

That perspective has guided my work across philanthropic, multilateral, and nonprofit environments. I operate in the space between strategy and execution, where priorities need to translate into something that actually holds across teams and over time.

Over nine years, I’ve moved from coordinating projects to shaping the systems behind them. I focus on how operational, relational, and strategic layers connect, and I design the structures that allow organizations to work more clearly, consistently, and effectively.

I bring a strong systems instinct and a strategic lens. I see where things are not connecting, and I turn that into processes, tools, or ways of working that make the whole system function better.

"Real change happens in the spaces where systems, people, and decisions meet."
